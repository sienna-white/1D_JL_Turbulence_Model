1 """
2 DataFrameRow{<:AbstractDataFrame, <:AbstractIndex}
3
4 A view of one row of an `AbstractDataFrame`.
5
6 A `DataFrameRow` is returned by `getindex` or `view` functions when one row and a
7 selection of columns are requested, or when iterating the result
8 of the call to the [`eachrow`](@ref) function.
9
10 The `DataFrameRow` constructor can also be called directly:
11
12 ```
13 DataFrameRow(parent::AbstractDataFrame, row::Integer, cols=:)
14 ```
15
16 A `DataFrameRow` supports the iteration interface and can therefore be passed to
17 functions that expect a collection as an argument. Its element type is always `Any`.
18
19 Indexing is one-dimensional like specifying a column of a `DataFrame`.
20 You can also access the data in a `DataFrameRow` using the `getproperty` and
21 `setproperty!` functions and convert it to a `Tuple`, `NamedTuple`, or `Vector`
22 using the corresponding functions.
23
24 If the selection of columns in a parent data frame is passed as `:` (a colon)
25 then `DataFrameRow` will always have all columns from the parent,
26 even if they are added or removed after its creation.
27
28 # Examples
29 ```jldoctest
30 julia> df = DataFrame(a=repeat([1, 2], outer=[2]),
31 b=repeat(["a", "b"], inner=[2]),
32 c=1:4)
33 4×3 DataFrame
34 Row │ a b c
35 │ Int64 String Int64
36 ─────┼──────────────────────
37 1 │ 1 a 1
38 2 │ 2 a 2
39 3 │ 1 b 3
40 4 │ 2 b 4
41
42 julia> df[1, :]
43 DataFrameRow
44 Row │ a b c
45 │ Int64 String Int64
46 ─────┼──────────────────────
47 1 │ 1 a 1
48
49 julia> @view df[end, [:a]]
50 DataFrameRow
51 Row │ a
52 │ Int64
53 ─────┼───────
54 4 │ 2
55
56 julia> eachrow(df)[1]
57 DataFrameRow
58 Row │ a b c
59 │ Int64 String Int64
60 ─────┼──────────────────────
61 1 │ 1 a 1
62
63 julia> Tuple(df[1, :])
64 (1, "a", 1)
65
66 julia> NamedTuple(df[1, :])

68
69 julia> Vector(df[1, :])

72 "a"
73 1
